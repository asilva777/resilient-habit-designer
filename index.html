The Behavior Design Model, developed by BJ Fogg, simplifies behavior change into a formula: B = MAP, where B represents Behavior, M stands for Motivation, A stands for Ability, and P stands for Prompt.

  • This formula encapsulates the core components of how behavior change occurs.
  • To stop a habit or unwanted behavior: reverse the formula to B = M - A - P. Making all these three (3) factors difficult to act upon can effectively disrupt the behavior.
